Data Analytics Basics - Corporate course

Course Description

This course is designed for beginner analysts who want to learn how to work with data in Excel. Over the course of 2 months, you will study the essential Excel functions: working with tables, formulas, filtering, conditional formatting, and visualization. The course combines theory and practice, allowing you to immediately apply your knowledge to real-world data analysis. This course is suitable for corporate employees, students, and professionals looking to enhance their skills.

What Will You Learn in This Course?

In this course, you will master advanced data manipulation techniques in Excel, including creating complex tables, using formulas, and working with filtering tools. You will also learn how to effectively visualize data and create professional reports that simplify data analysis.

The practical part of the course includes working with real-world data, where you will learn how to identify and correct errors, as well as conduct detailed data analysis. This experience will help you prepare for learning more advanced analytical tools such as SQL and Python.

Why Is This Course Useful?

  • Practical Focus – Emphasis on solving real-world problems and working on projects.

  • Adaptability – The course is adjusted to match the students' skill levels.

  • Universality – Excel skills are essential in various industries.

By the end of the course, you will be able to confidently work with Excel and use it for data analysis in your professional activities. This course serves as a foundation for learning more complex tools such as SQL or Python and supports your career growth in the field of data analysis.

Course Curriculum

This course will help you learn Excel for data analysis, including working with tables, formulas, filtering, and visualization. You will study how to create professional reports, correct errors, and apply Excel to solve real-world tasks. The course combines theory and practice, laying the foundation for further learning in data analysis.

  • Formula elements: operators, constants, and references.
  • Order of operations in calculations.
  • Types of references: absolute, relative, and mixed.

  • Data sorting and filtering.
  • Data processing.
  • Data validation.

  • Creating charts.
  • Formatting charts.
  • Conditional formatting.
